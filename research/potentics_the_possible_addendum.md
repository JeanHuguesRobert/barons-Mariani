# Potentics Addendum — The Possible versus possibilities

This addendum clarifies a distinction that should be merged into `research/potentics.md`.

---

## 1. The distinction

Potentics should use **The Possible** as its doctrinal term, not merely *possibilities*.

The distinction is essential:

```text
Possibilities are what a situation seems to offer.
The Possible is what reality has not forbidden.
```

The plural *possibilities* tends to refer to a local, visible, contingent set of options. It suggests what appears available to a person, institution, territory, market, scientific community, technical system, or historical moment.

**The Possible**, capitalized, names the wider field of what reality permits, including what has not yet been seen, named, funded, institutionalized, socially accepted, technically assembled, or cognitively recognized.

---

## 2. Why it matters

If Potentics is only the rational exploration of *possibilities*, it may be misunderstood as a method for choosing among visible options.

That is too small.

Potentics is concerned with a larger domain:

```text
visible possibilities
latent possibilities
blocked possibilities
forgotten possibilities
unrecognized possibilities
physically possible but institutionally prevented states
technically possible but socially unseen states
socially possible but cognitively unnamed states
```

This is why **The Possible** is stronger.

It goes down to the deep constraint layer: what is logically, physically, biologically, technically, institutionally, socially, or economically capable of becoming actual under some configuration of conditions and effort.

---

## 3. Relation to potentiality

The existing Potentics distinction between possibility and potentiality remains valid:

```text
possibility  = binary admission into the possible domain
potentiality = scalar propensity of a possible to actualize
```

This addendum adds a higher-level clarification:

```text
possibilities = situated visible options
the possible  = abstract domain of what can be
The Possible  = doctrinal term for the full field of what reality permits
potentiality  = graded measure of a possible's propensity to actualize
```

In short:

```text
The Possible contains possibles.
Some possibles become visible as possibilities.
Some possibilities have potentiality.
Potentics evaluates, cultivates, tests, inhibits, or abandons potentialities.
```

---

## 4. Relation to physics and reality

The term **The Possible** should remain anchored in reality.

It does not mean fantasy, wish, preference, ideology, or narrative desire. It includes only what is not forbidden by the relevant constraints of reality.

Those constraints may include:

```text
logic
known physical law
biological limits
thermodynamic costs
material availability
technical feasibility
institutional structure
coordination capacity
social acceptance
learning rate
time windows
reversibility
```

A possible may be physically permitted but institutionally blocked. It may be technically feasible but socially invisible. It may be economically possible only after coordination failures are solved.

Potentics studies these layers rather than collapsing them into a single yes/no judgment.

---

## 5. Working formulas

```text
The Possible is larger than the visible set of possibilities.
```

```text
Possibilities are what a situation seems to offer. The Possible is what reality has not forbidden.
```

```text
Potentics is not the art of choosing among available options. It is the rational exploration of The Possible.
```

```text
A blocked possible is not an impossible. It is a possible whose path of actualization is inhibited.
```

```text
A society becomes capable when it learns to distinguish the impossible from the merely untried, unseen, blocked, or badly coordinated.
```
