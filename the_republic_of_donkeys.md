### Abstract

This working paper presents the “Republic of Donkeys,” an ongoing, small-scale, place-based experiment in commons governance. The project tests whether a minimal, non-speculative, and deeply local organizational system can enable effective collective decision-making and resource management without relying on centralized authority or complex institutional design.

By combining a heuristic device (the “donkey”), a small group of participants, lightweight physical anchors, and iterative governance practices, the experiment explores how functional coordination can emerge from simple rules applied *in situ*. Early observations suggest that minimal, adaptive structures grounded in real-world conditions can support stable micro-scale governance. This paper outlines the conceptual basis, methodology, initial setup, and preliminary observations while deliberately limiting the disclosure of sensitive operational details.

---

### 1. Introduction

Modern governance systems frequently suffer from excessive complexity, detachment from local realities, and poor adaptability. At the same time, scholarship on the commons and decentralized coordination has demonstrated the potential of locally rooted, collectively managed systems.

The Republic of Donkeys is a situated field experiment that seeks to test, under real conditions, whether effective governance can emerge from minimal structures and evolve through practice rather than top-down institutional engineering. It is not a theoretical model, but a living experiment.

---

### 2. Conceptual Framework

#### 2.1 Main Traditions

The project draws pragmatically on commons governance (Ostrom, 1990), subsidiarity and polycentricity (Ostrom, 2010), prefigurative politics, and place-based experimentation. These serve as orienting references rather than rigid frameworks.

#### 2.2 The “Donkey” as Heuristic and Material Device

The Corsican donkey operates on three interconnected levels:  
- **Symbolic and patrimonial**: In Corsica, the donkey (“u sumeru corsu”) carries an immense emotional and cultural charge. Saving the Corsican donkey is, in a very real sense, an attempt to help preserve the Corsican soul — a living link to an agropastoral memory that today mobilizes less than 2 % of the active population yet remains a powerful marker of identity.  
- **Practical**: As a real animal on site, it imposes biological rhythms and material constraints while becoming a non-human member of the commons.  
- **Political**: The presence of the donkey anchors the experiment in a concrete practice of *ascending subsidiarity* — bottom-up legitimacy against Parisian centralism as well as against certain local elected officials who are often reluctant to accept strict transparency and real power-sharing.

Animal and human well-being are treated as one single, non-negotiable constraint.

---

### 3. Core Hypothesis

> A small-scale, locally embedded, non-speculative organizational system based on participant equality and iterative decision-making can generate functional governance outcomes without centralized control.

**This hypothesis rests on a foundational postulate**: political equality, despite its imperfections and inherent risks, remains the most robust model and the one least prone to power capture by a minority at the expense of the inhabitants.

---

### 4. Methodology

- *In situ* experimentation (early stage)  
- Small founding group (initial triad)  
- Iterative rule-making with rapid feedback loops  
- Participant-observation  
- Deliberate constraints: limited scale, partial documentation, reversibility  

Validation prioritizes real-world functionality over theoretical elegance. At this fragile early stage, the continuation of the project will largely depend on the initiator’s capacity to navigate inevitable obstacles.

---

### 5. Initial Configuration

**Location**: Micro-territory in the Corte region of Corsica — mixed urban-rural environment.  
**Participants**: Founding group of three with fully shared decision-making and fluid roles.  
**Material & Living Anchors**: Light land access, minimal infrastructure, and the physical presence of a Corsican donkey.  
**Provisional Revenue Model**: Dual source — visitors interested in slow tourism and locals with storage/service needs.

---

### 6. Operational Principles

- Radical equality in decision-making (postulate of direct democracy)  
- Strong ascending subsidiarity  
- Non-speculative relationship to resources  
- Preference for use-rights over ownership concentration  
- Continuous, iterative rule formation  

---

### 7. Preliminary Observations

- Functional coordination emerges with very little formalization  
- Physical and living anchors significantly stabilize group dynamics  
- Local constraints shape behavior more effectively than abstract rules  
- The donkey heuristic facilitates an egalitarian culture  

---

### 8. Discussion

#### 8.1 Limits
Very small sample size, high dependence on interpersonal alignment, lack of longitudinal data.

#### 8.2 Tensions
Informal practice versus existing legal frameworks; necessary opacity versus transparency; narrative power versus analytical rigor.

#### 8.3 Blind Spots and Methodological Limitations (Frank Assessment)

This micro-experiment is deliberately modest in size yet carries ambitious longer-term implications. It is, at heart, one more episode in the perennial struggle between the people and the elites — a concrete attempt to rebalance power at the most local level.

Key acknowledged blind spots include:  
- Scaling will only be considered after multiple successful iterations. If the model proves robust, it could inspire diffusion to other communes, including the reopening of historic mule paths to promote a “slow life” and a differentiated form of slow tourism that helps Corsica move away from mass tourism serving rentier interests.  
- The strong symbolic and emotional investment in the Corsican donkey is a major asset of sympathy and legitimacy, but also carries the risk of romanticization.  
- The postulate of radical equality accepts the associated risks; informal power dynamics will be monitored explicitly.  
- The project remains fragile at this early stage and depends heavily on the initiator’s effectiveness in the face of legal, social, and economic obstacles.

These limitations are not hidden; they are integral to the experimental ethos.

---

### 9. Implications

If further iterations confirm these patterns, the experiment suggests that governance can be prototyped effectively at micro scale, that distributed systems benefit from strong physical and living anchors, and that minimal rulesets often outperform complex frameworks in early stages. Longer-term, it may contribute to a broader practice of ascending subsidiarity and to a more singular, autonomous Corsica that is less dependent on mass tourism.

No generalization is claimed at this point.

### 11. Evolution Path (Provisional)

- Stabilization of the initial site(s)  
- Gradual and careful inclusion of new participants  
- Exploration of light legal structures  
- Controlled documentation  
- Potential future diffusion (mule paths, slow-life networks) **only after proven iterations**

---

### 12. Conclusion

The Republic of Donkeys is a modest, grounded attempt to explore how governance can emerge from minimal, egalitarian, and iterative structures rooted in specific places and real relationships. It does not offer a universal model, but a living process open to observation, critique — and, if successful, to careful adaptation and wider resonance.
